Technical analysis

Trading, like any other activity, requires a systematic approach. Understanding the levels of opening and closing positions is only one of the aspects.

Forex technical analysis

It comes down to finding the most suitable points to detect in the market in order to “buy cheaper, find” - this can be the detection of trend reversal points, determining whether its direction is suitable for entering the market at the time of discovery, or, for example, detecting the discovery of opportunities. to earn even during the flat period. On an ascending or bullish trader, you can earn both on an increase in the price of a currency pair and on temporary rollbacks (corrections). They use manual closing, Stop Loss orders (fixing losses) and Take Profit (profits). The search for points of view is based on the same rules of technical analysis.

Basic postulates of technical analysis

For a simple application of technical analysis of the trading period from the beginning of trading on Monday to its completion in a high degree at the level of gaps - time frames. Thanks to them, it is possible to analyze the most significant parameters. These include the price at the time of opening / closing the period, price minimum / maximum, volumes.

The basic postulates of technical analysis are the basis of almost any trading strategy

  • Prices include everything

    Their change is influenced by absolutely any political or economic factor (sometimes climatic). The degree of influence is due to the significance for the state, the currency of which is traded.

  • Subordination of prices to trends

    The value of currencies is constantly changing. As a result, currency pairs then grow up, then fall in price. In certain periods, a flat may occur - a time of uncertainty, when the price does not change much over time.

  • History repeats itself

    A trend reversal, a flat state of the market is observed at the same price levels. Once noticed, a trend can be repeated many times, which traders use in trading systems.

Standard and non-
standard timeframes

The trading platform offers several standard time frames - from M1 (minute) to MN (month). The chart is divided into nine time frames, in addition to the extreme ones, M5, M15, M30, H1, H4, D1, W1 are used. The dynamics of visual price changes depends on the choice of the period. Short-term trading systems use time intervals no older than M30. Sometimes traders resort to the formation of non-standard time frames. Their task is to compensate for the shortcomings of typical values. For example, if the indicator on H1 displays a late value, and on M30 it is “in a hurry”. Presumably "average value", i.e. M45 timeframe should show the most accurate time to open trades.

Technical analysis tools

Periodic repetition of previously occurring events allows you to prepare for them and, in a timely manner, identifying a tendency to return to previous positions, earn profit. A trader has a lot of tools in his arsenal: support/resistance levels, ascending and descending channels. The terminal has built-in functions for drawing the necessary lines on the chart of a currency pair, access to them is provided from the "Graphic Tools" panel.

In addition to the standard lines, tools are offered:

  • Grid, fan, arcs, time zones, Fibonacci channel
  • Grid, lines, Gann fan.
  • Fourier series

The listed trading "add-ons" are an attempt to mathematically predict the subsequent price movement based on the technical analysis of the previous trend dynamics in the market. The use of channel instruments allows you to trade at the moments of price reversal within the channel, taking into account the current trend. Channel lines indicate where a correction is most likely to reverse, if the price moves against the trend, support/resistance lines show the price level where a trend change is likely, the beginning of a protracted correction. All technical analysis tools are based on mathematical calculations and are used mainly as a basis for determining trends, and more precisely, the moment of entering the market is determined by indicators.

The use of Japanese
candlesticks

Forex technical analysis includes control of such components of Japanese candlesticks as:

  • The body of the candle. The color indicates the direction of price movement. By default, white is used for rising candles and black for falling candles.

  • The size of the candle. It indicates the strength of the pressure of sellers / buyers. There are Doji candles when the opening price is equal to the closing price.

  • The presence of a tail and its size. Displays the indecisiveness of sellers / buyers in the market, which leads to price fluctuations around a constant level.

Figures are formed from various types of candles, their combination can be used to determine the current trend (trend), an approaching reversal. In the absence of certainty on the candle of the H1 period, the trader switches to lower time frames and thus provides a more accurate Forex technical analysis. When it comes to scalping, they act the other way around - they work mainly on younger TFs, and look at the general trend on older ones.

Candlesticks also display the volatility of a currency pair. If you switch to periods from H4 and above, it will become clear in what range the price is moving in the current month or this week, is there a risk of a reversal, the market entering a long-term flat.

Graphic shapes

10 basic figures of technical analysis are applied here

  • Triangle. There is bullish, bearish and symmetrical (the latter means a continuation of the previous trend).

  • Diamond. Visually, a rhombus is formed on the chart, the tops of which rest against the resistance/support levels.

  • Double top. A reversal pattern suitable for use both as a separate instrument and as an additional signal.

  • Wedge. One of the "long-playing" figures can be formed over a long period, which is convenient for use in long-term trading strategies.

  • Pennant. A flag-like pattern that has a similar definition.

  • Triple bottom. Allows you to determine the direction of the breakdown during the flat period.

  • Triple top. One of the tools for determining the trend reversal point, which works at the time of consolidation.

  • Double bottom. Another figure indicating a change in the downward trend to an upward one.

  • Flag. It is observed after news impulses, indicates its continuation in a previously determined direction.

  • Saucer. A figure used by lovers of long-term trading.

Types of indicators

Indicators are conditionally divided into several categories:

  • Trendy. They show the direction, strength of the trend, the probability of a reversal.

  • Oscillators. Display a mathematical model for measuring the rate of change in the market.

  • Bill Williams and Volumes. They work on a "separate theory", but have a fairly high efficiency in short-term and long-term trading.

The key task of indicators is to indicate the recommended direction for entering the market (Buy or Sell orders), showing volatility for profit forecasting. Without their use, the trader will have to focus on "simpler" signals - support/resistance levels, trend channel lines.

Many trading strategies contain rules for combining the readings of different indicators. When conditions appear on one of them (conditionally the main one), the trader looks for confirmation on others.
